Taj Mahal, Agra:

Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, The Taj Mahal stands as a symbol of enduring love's plea. Commissioned in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, Its construction involved thousands, a labor monumental.

Qutub Minar, Delhi:

Qutub Minar, a towering symbol of Delhi's past, Erected by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, a conqueror steadfast. Started in 1193, its construction faced many a twist, With successive rulers adding layers amidst.

Golden Temple, Amritsar:

Founded by Guru Ram Das, the fourth Sikh Guru revered, Its foundation laid in 1577, a vision revered. A symbol of equality and spirituality's light, The temple's gold-plated dome shines bright.

Hampi, Karnataka:

Once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire's sway, Hampi's ruins now tell tales of its glory's display. Flourishing from the 14th to the 16th century's prime, Its temples, palaces, and bazaars stood sublime.

Varanasi (Benares):

Nestled on the banks of the sacred Ganges' flow, Varanasi's history stretches far, a city aglow. Revered as one of the oldest continuously inhabited places, Its ghats, temples, and alleys echo with spiritual traces.
